The California Poppy

California's state flower has many nicknames, including the California Poppy and the Golden Poppy. These beautiful flowers adorn California's rolling hills. It's one of the earliest wild flowers to grow in gardens. Every year on April 6th, California Poppy Day is celebrated.

Kentucky's Goldenrod

The goldenrod was named Kentucky's official state flower in 1926. The goldenrod has clusters of bright yellow flowers. It was used for medicinal purposes, like kidney stones and bladder infections, for centuries. Its genus name is Solidago.

Louisiana's Magnolia

The magnolia was named Louisiana's state flower in 1900. The tree grows in the southeastern U.S. and is also called the evergreen magnolia. This could be attested it its leathery, evergreen leaves.

New York's Big Rose

The rose is a very common flower, given on holidays like Valentine's Day or sold at almost any place flowers are sold. But it is special to New York because it was designated as the official state flower in 1955. It serves as a symbol of love and beauty. The rose is also the United States's official flower.
